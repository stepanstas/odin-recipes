Potato Pancakes

Description

Potato pancakes are one of my favorite foods that bring up many wonderful memories of growing up in the Czech Republic. Potato pancakes are very tasty and simple to make. They are made from raw shredded potatoes, egg, flour, garlic, and hot milk, and they are pan-fried in a fat your choice. My recommendation is to use some kind of stable cooking fat with high smoke point such as butter/ghee, lard, tallow/suet, palm or coconut oil instead of the pro-inflammatory vegetable oils.

Ingredients

  • Yellow potatoes like Yukon gold
  • All-purpose flour
  • Salt
  • Egg
  • Dried marjoram
  • Garlic: fresh, peeled and pressed or mashed
  • Milk: for pouring over shredded potatoes to prevent them from browning
  • Butter, lard or any cooking oil of your choice

Steps

  1. Peel the raw potatoes and shred them finely with a box grater.
  2. Transfer the grated potatoes to a colander and squeeze out the excess liquid they released.
  3. Put the squeezed potatoes in a bowl, pour hot milk over them. Add the flour, egg, pressed garlic, dried marjoram, and salt.
  4. Stir the potato batter into a semi-fluid consistency.
  5. Take a non-stick frying pan, put the cooking fat/oil in it (1-2 Tablespoons per pancake), and heat it up to medium heat.
  6. Mix the potato batter evenly before frying, scoop one ladleful and carefully place it in the middle of the preheated pan. Using a flat spoon, spread the dough into a Â¼ inch (5-6 mm) thick pancake about 8 inches (20 cm) in diameter.
  7. Fry each potato pancake for about 2-3 minutes on both sides. Flip with a flat spatula once the edges start to turn golden.
  8. Stack up the finished potato pancakes on a plate. If you want to save calories and make the pancakes less fatty, line them with paper towels to soak up excess fat.
  9. Serve and enjoy!
